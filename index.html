The Meaning

What is the Honey?

Honey is the sweetness we create — for our customers, our teams, ourselves, and our future.

To the Customer

Sweet experiences. The "wow" moments. The relief when something just works. The stories they tell their neighbors.

To the Team

Fulfillment. Pride in craft. The feeling of being part of something meaningful. "I helped that family."

To Ourselves

Growth. Purpose. The satisfaction of doing work that matters. Becoming the person we're meant to be.

To the Business

Profitability. Growth. Sustainability. The resources to keep investing in the hive and our people.

The Philosophy

The Framework of Flourishing

Business is a tool for human flourishing. Serving the customer is not a transaction; it is a high privilege and a covenant to leave a legacy of meaning.

The Journey

2026 Roadmap

A year-long commitment to accountability, excellence, and human flourishing.

February Kickoff

Perfecting the Comb

Establishing the Brand Promise as our North Star and living up to it internally first. How dispatch, sales, technicians, and everyone else along the way serve one another as internal customers to ensure a seamless journey. Building a culture of healthy accountability through caring personally while challenging directly.

Q2 Session

The Pollination

Treating every home visit as a flourishing opportunity. Using the Shep Hyken "Moment of Magic" framework to transform standard service into memorable experiences. What does effortless feel like for our customers? Identifying and eliminating the friction points we control.

Q3 Session

The Sweetness We Leave Behind

Legacy and advocacy. How do we turn satisfied customers into advocates who tell our story? Empowerment to say yes, treating every individual with dignity, and recovering gracefully when things go wrong. This is about the aftertaste — what lingers after we leave.

Q4 Capstone — Marble Falls

The Honey Harvest

The Annual Manager's Training in Marble Falls, TX. Three sessions across Tuesday and Wednesday bringing Austin, Dallas, and Houston together under one roof. Content that builds on the year's foundation, cross-market collaboration on best practices, and storytelling sessions where teams share what they implemented and what worked. This is where we harvest the stories that define who we are.

For Discussion

Questions for the Ownership Group

To help shape the 2026 curriculum, I'd love your input on the following:

Question 1

What do our managers struggle with most — internal handoffs or external delivery?

If it's internal, we lean Q1 heavier. If it's external, Q2 and Q3 need more depth.

Question 2

What's the biggest gap between your Brand Promise and your actual customer experience?

That gap tells us where to focus Q2 and Q3 content.

Question 3

What do we want them to be able to DO differently by Q4?

We'll work backward from that skill or behavior to design each quarter.

Question 4

Are there any specific books, frameworks, videos, or "hooks" you've used in the past that your teams have loved — beyond what we've introduced in previous trainings?

We can anchor each quarter to the resources that resonate most with your culture.
